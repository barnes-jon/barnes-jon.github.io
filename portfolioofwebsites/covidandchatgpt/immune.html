Your Immune System and inflammation

Inflammation is a process that occurs in response to tissue damage or infection. It is a complex process that involves the activation of various immune cells and the release of chemicals called cytokines, which help to stimulate an immune response and repair damaged tissue.

The inflammation cycle in the immune system can be described as follows:

  • Stimulus: The inflammation cycle is triggered by a stimulus, such as tissue damage, infection, or an allergen.
  • Vasodilation: When the stimulus is detected, blood vessels in the affected area dilate (expand), which allows more blood to flow to the site of the inflammation.
  • Leukocyte recruitment: Immune cells called leukocytes (white blood cells) are attracted to the site of the inflammation. Leukocytes are able to move from the blood vessels into the tissues through a process called extravasation.
  • Inflammatory response: Once they reach the site of the inflammation, leukocytes release chemicals called cytokines, which help to stimulate an immune response and repair damaged tissue. Cytokines also attract more leukocytes to the site of the inflammation.
  • Resolution: If the stimulus is successfully resolved, the inflammation cycle comes to an end and the affected tissues return to their normal state. If the stimulus is not resolved, the inflammation cycle can become chronic and lead to ongoing inflammation and tissue damage.
